About

I am currently studying an MSc in Computer Science with Artificial Intelligence part-time. I have completed all taught modules, averaging 86%, and I am now working on my dissertation, which will be completed in November 2026 before I graduate.

For my dissertation, I am developing a production-focused machine learning system that predicts when logistics assets in a UK-wide supply chain are likely to become lost. The project uses IoT tracker data from roll cages moving between warehouses, distribution centres, and stores to learn subtle movement patterns that are hard to detect manually, with the goal of surfacing at-risk assets early enough for operations teams to intervene and prevent loss.

The system is designed as a human-in-the-loop learning pipeline: operational experts regularly label asset status, this labelled data is fed back into the dataset, and models retrain automatically with updated performance metrics. Across my dissertation and other projects, my focus is on applied machine learning and data science that deliver real commercial impact, with clear structure and reproducible results.
